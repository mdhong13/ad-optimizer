One Message is stored on Amazon Web Services (AWS)

It is a message that is automatically sent to the recipient when the timer you set expires.

Even if your smartphone is turned off,
Even if your smartphone is damaged or falls into the sea, One Message will still be sent to the recipient.

One Message is sent to 100% of the recipients.

SMS is not secure.

SMS on smartphones cannot be encrypted. Do not include the private key of your cryptocurrency wallet in the message. Instead, leave instructions on where the private key is stored.

"I left you a gift. Open Carl Sagan's Cosmos in Dad's study."

"The private key for the Bitcoin wallet is hidden in the sock drawer, inside the bundle of mismatched socks. There are 100 of them. You are rich now."

Timer reset

When the One Message app detects smartphone usage, it sends a check-in signal to the server and resets the timer.
The One Message delivery timer can be set for up to 7 days. Each time you use your smartphone, the timer resets to 7 days, and the message will not be sent.

To keep the app running in the background, please add it as an exception to automatic power-saving settings in your smartphone's permission settings.
If the app cannot detect smartphone usage in power-saving mode, it will send a notification. Tap the notification to open the app. The app will then send a check-in signal to the server and reset the timer.

If you reinstall the app or change your smartphone

Set an emergency recovery password in Settings.
If you reinstall One Message and enter the recovery password, you can restore your saved messages.

If you do not have a recovery password or have lost it

Do not worry. If you install the app and register your existing phone number, all messages associated with that phone number will be deleted and will not be sent.
